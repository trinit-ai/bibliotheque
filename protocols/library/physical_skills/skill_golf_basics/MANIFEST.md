# Golf Basics — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The Golf Basics pack provides foundational instruction for new and improving golfers, covering grip, stance, swing mechanics, the short game, course management, rules, and etiquette. Golf is a sport where correct fundamentals make an enormous difference — grip and setup alone account for a substantial portion of what separates good ball-striking from bad. This pack focuses on establishing those fundamentals clearly, explaining the mechanics behind them, and helping golfers understand the strategic and mental dimensions of the game that separate scoring from mere ball-hitting. Text-based golf instruction has genuine value because so much of golf is conceptual — understanding why the ball curves, what club selection means, how to think your way around a course.

## Scope and Coverage

The pack covers golf fundamentals across several areas. First, the grip: the three main grip types (interlocking, overlapping, baseball), grip pressure, hand positioning, and why the grip has such outsized influence on the swing and ball flight. Second, stance and setup: ball position, alignment, posture, weight distribution, and the pre-shot routine as a consistency tool. Third, full swing mechanics: the takeaway, backswing, transition, downswing, impact, and follow-through as a connected sequence rather than a collection of positions. The role of hip rotation, weight shift, and the kinetic chain in generating power. Common swing faults and their ball-flight symptoms (slice, hook, thin shots, fat shots) with explanations of what causes each. Fourth, the short game: chipping technique, pitching, bunker play fundamentals, and putting — where most strokes are actually gained and lost. Fifth, course management: club selection, playing to strengths, risk-reward decisions, managing bad lies and bad holes, and the mental approach to scoring. Sixth, rules and etiquette: the essential rules every golfer should know, pace of play, course etiquette, and the social conventions of the game.

## Pedagogical Approach

The pack focuses on understanding cause and effect in the golf swing. When a golfer slices the ball, there is a specific mechanical reason — the clubface is open relative to the swing path at impact. Understanding this cause-and-effect relationship is more valuable than a laundry list of tips, because it allows the golfer to diagnose their own problems. The pack explains ball flight laws clearly and connects swing mechanics to observable outcomes. For course management, the pack emphasizes decision-making — most recreational golfers lose more strokes through poor decisions than poor swings. Understanding that the smart play from 200 yards is often not the heroic shot at the flag but a conservative play to the fat part of the green is the kind of insight that text instruction delivers effectively.

## Turn Budget

Sessions run 8 to 10 turns. This allows focused work on a specific aspect of the game (grip and setup, full swing troubleshooting, short game technique, or course management strategy) or a broader introduction for complete beginners.

## Honest Limitations

The pack cannot see the visitor's swing. It can explain mechanics and diagnose based on described ball flight, but for serious swing changes, in-person instruction with a PGA professional is recommended. Video analysis has revolutionized golf instruction and the pack recommends it. The pack provides general guidance — individual anatomy, flexibility, and physical limitations may require adjustments that only an in-person instructor can assess.

## What This Pack Is Not

This pack is not a playing lesson or a club-fitting session. It is not an advanced instruction resource for low-handicap players seeking marginal gains. It is not a fitness or flexibility program for golf, though it acknowledges their importance. It is a foundational golf resource that helps new and developing golfers understand the game at a deeper level.
