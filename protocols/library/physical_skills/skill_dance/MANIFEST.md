# Dance Fundamentals — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The Dance Fundamentals pack provides a conceptual foundation for partner and social dance forms including salsa, tango, swing, and waltz. Dance is inherently physical and musical — it happens in bodies moving through space to music — which makes it one of the more challenging skills to address through text. This pack is honest about that limitation while delivering genuine value in the areas where text excels: understanding musical structure and counting, learning the theory behind lead-follow dynamics, grasping the cultural and historical context of different dance forms, and building the conceptual framework that makes physical learning faster and more intentional.

## Scope and Coverage

The pack covers four major social dance forms along with the underlying principles that connect them. First, salsa: the basic timing (on-1 and on-2), the concept of the basic step, cross-body lead, turn patterns, the relationship between the dancer and the clave rhythm, and the distinction between LA-style, New York-style, and Cuban-style salsa. Second, tango: Argentine tango fundamentals including the embrace, walking as the foundation, the concept of the axis, the mark and the follow, musicality in tango (how the dance interprets the music differently from other forms), and the improvisational nature of social tango. Third, swing: East Coast Swing and Lindy Hop, the triple-step and rock-step foundation, swing-outs, the bounce and pulse that define the style, and the relationship between swing dance and swing music. Fourth, waltz: the box step, rise and fall, the 3/4 time signature, Viennese waltz versus slow waltz, and the rotational mechanics that make waltz distinctive. Across all styles, the pack covers universal partner dance concepts: lead-follow communication through frame and connection, musical phrasing and how to dance to the music rather than just on the beat, floorcraft and social dance etiquette, and the progression from patterns to improvisation.

## Pedagogical Approach

The pack focuses on what text can actually convey about dance. It excels at explaining musical structure — how to hear and count the rhythm in salsa music, how to recognize the phrasing in a tango orchestra, where the accent falls in swing music. It can explain the theory of leading and following — how signals travel through connection, why frame matters, the difference between leading a movement and pushing a partner. It can describe the cultural context and history that enriches understanding of each dance form. What it cannot do is teach someone to dance. The pack is clear about this. Physical dance requires a physical environment — a dance floor, music, and ideally a partner and an instructor. The pack provides the intellectual foundation that makes that physical learning more effective and more enjoyable.

## Turn Budget

Sessions run 8 to 10 turns. This allows focused exploration of one dance style in depth or a comparative overview of multiple styles to help a visitor choose what to pursue.

## Honest Limitations

Dance is perhaps the physical skill least amenable to text instruction. The pack acknowledges this early and often. It cannot demonstrate movements, correct timing issues the visitor cannot hear, or provide the physical feedback of connection with a partner. What it can do — and this is genuinely valuable — is provide the conceptual and musical framework that makes class time more productive. A beginning salsa dancer who already understands on-1 timing and the concept of cross-body lead before their first class will learn significantly faster than one who does not.

## What This Pack Is Not

This pack is not a dance class. It does not replace instruction. It is not a performance coaching session or a competition preparation tool. It is not a comprehensive history of dance, though historical context enriches understanding. It is a conceptual companion to physical dance learning — the theory that makes practice smarter.
