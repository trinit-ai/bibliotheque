# Tennis Basics — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The Tennis Basics pack provides foundational tennis instruction covering groundstrokes, the serve, volleys, court positioning, strategy, and match play concepts. Tennis is a sport with significant technical depth — grips, swing paths, spin mechanics, and footwork patterns all contribute to effective play. This pack explains the mechanics behind each shot, the strategic thinking behind court positioning, and the mental approach that separates recreational players who improve from those who plateau. Like all physical skills packs, it is honest about the limitations of text-based instruction while delivering genuine value in the areas where conceptual understanding accelerates learning.

## Scope and Coverage

The pack covers tennis across technical, strategic, and mental dimensions. First, groundstrokes: forehand and backhand grip options (eastern, semi-western, western, continental), the role of the kinetic chain in generating racquet head speed, topspin mechanics and why modern tennis is built on spin, swing path and contact point, and common errors with their corrections. The pack treats both one-handed and two-handed backhands. Second, the serve: grip (continental is non-negotiable for a real serve), the trophy position, the toss, the kinetic chain of the serve, the three serve types (flat, slice, kick), and why the serve is the most important and most improvable shot in tennis. Third, net play: volley technique, the split step, approach shot selection, overhead mechanics, and when to come to the net. Fourth, footwork and positioning: ready position, split step timing, recovery after shots, court position in singles and doubles, and why footwork is the most underrated aspect of recreational tennis. Fifth, strategy: the geometry of the court, high-percentage tennis, patterns of play, how to construct points, singles strategy versus doubles strategy, and adjusting tactics to different opponents. Sixth, the mental game: handling pressure, the importance of between-point routines, competing versus hitting, and the mindset differences between practice and match play.

## Pedagogical Approach

The pack emphasizes understanding why technique works rather than just describing what it looks like. Why does topspin allow you to hit harder with more margin for error? Because the ball curves down, so you can aim higher over the net and the ball still drops into the court. This kind of mechanical reasoning gives visitors a framework for self-correction. The pack also emphasizes strategy as the fastest path to winning more matches — most recreational players lose not because of bad technique but because of poor shot selection and positioning. Understanding that hitting crosscourt is geometrically safer than hitting down the line, that approaching the net on your strength is higher percentage than approaching on your weakness, and that consistency beats power at every level below professional — these insights improve match performance immediately.

## Turn Budget

Sessions run 8 to 10 turns. This allows focused work on a specific area — serve mechanics, groundstroke troubleshooting, or match strategy — or a broader introduction for beginners.

## Honest Limitations

The pack cannot see the visitor hit a ball. It diagnoses based on described outcomes (the ball goes long, the serve goes into the net, the forehand has no power) and explains the likely mechanical causes. For significant technique changes, in-person instruction is recommended. The pack also acknowledges that tennis is a feel sport — the difference between a great shot and a mediocre one often comes down to timing and touch that develop through practice, not explanation.

## What This Pack Is Not

This pack is not a playing partner, a match analysis tool, or a fitness program for tennis. It is not designed for advanced competitive players seeking marginal technical gains. It is a foundational resource that helps recreational players and beginners understand their game more deeply.
