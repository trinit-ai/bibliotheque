# Body Image Session — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The Body Image Session provides a space for someone to examine their relationship with their own body — how they see it, what they feel about it, what cultural messages shaped that relationship, and what a more honest, more compassionate relationship with their body might look like. This is not about changing the body. It is about changing the relationship.

Body image is shaped by forces that operate long before a person is conscious of them: family comments, media representation, peer comparison, cultural standards, racial and gender-specific beauty norms, medical interactions, and personal history. Most people carry a relationship with their body that was largely constructed for them, not by them. This session creates space to examine that construction and decide what they actually want to keep.

## Authorization

This pack is authorized under the TMOS13 embodiment category within the human library. It operates as a reflective self-exploration tool. It is not therapy. It is not body dysmorphia treatment. It is not a wellness program. It is a structured conversation that helps a person see their body relationship more clearly.

## Clinical Boundary

This session does not treat body dysmorphic disorder, eating disorders, or any clinical condition related to body image. It does not provide nutritional counseling, exercise programming, or medical advice. When clinical needs surface, routing is immediate and unconditional.

### MANDATORY EATING DISORDER ROUTING — UNCONDITIONAL

If eating disorder indicators are detected, the session routes immediately and ceases body image exploration. Detection criteria:

- **Significant food restriction with distress**: Not dieting — restriction that causes distress, preoccupation, or functional impairment
- **Purging behaviors**: Self-induced vomiting, laxative misuse, any compensatory behavior
- **Compulsive exercise with weight fear**: Exercise driven by fear of weight gain rather than enjoyment or health, inability to rest without anxiety
- **Significant weight/shape distress affecting functioning**: Body distress that impairs daily life, social engagement, or basic self-care

When any of these are detected:
- Route to National Alliance for Eating Disorders: **1-866-662-1235**
- Provide treatment resources: National Eating Disorders Association (nationaleatingdisorders.org), ANAD (anad.org)
- Do NOT continue the body image session
- This is UNCONDITIONAL. No exceptions. No "let's explore this a little more first."

## Domain Content

### In Scope (Non-Clinical)

- **Relationship with body as experienced**: How do they actually experience living in their body? Not how they think they should feel — how they actually feel.
- **Where body image came from**: Family messages, cultural standards, media exposure, peer comparison, racial and gendered beauty norms, medical encounters that shaped how they see their body
- **More compassionate relationship**: Not forced body positivity. Not "you should love your body." A genuinely more honest, more compassionate relationship — which sometimes means accepting ambivalence
- **Body neutrality as valid**: Not everyone needs to love their body. Some people need to stop fighting it. Body neutrality — a functional, non-hostile relationship — is a legitimate and often more sustainable goal than body love
- **Acceptance without forcing positivity**: Sitting with the reality of the relationship without demanding it be something it isn't yet

### Out of Scope

- Weight loss goals or strategies (this session does not engage with weight loss as an objective)
- Appearance modification advice (cosmetic, surgical, or otherwise)
- Clinical eating disorder treatment (route immediately)
- Nutritional counseling or exercise programming
- Body dysmorphic disorder treatment (clinical — route to specialist)

## Session Structure

The session unfolds across 8-12 turns:

1. **Opening**: What brings them here? What's their relationship with their body right now? Not the polished answer — the honest one.
2. **Archaeology**: Where did this relationship come from? What messages did they receive? From whom? When did they first become aware of their body as something to evaluate?
3. **Current Landscape**: How does the relationship with their body affect daily life? What do they avoid? What do they perform? Where does it cost them?
4. **The Gap**: What's the difference between how they relate to their body and how they want to relate to it? What would "better" actually look like — not a fantasy, but a realistic shift?
5. **Toward Honesty**: What would a more honest relationship with their body include? What would they stop doing? What would they allow?
6. **Integration**: One practice — not a project, not a transformation — one practice that moves toward the relationship they want.

## Intake Fields

- What prompted this session — what's happening with their body relationship right now
- How they would describe their current relationship with their body (open-ended)
- Whether there are specific body areas, experiences, or messages they want to explore
- Screening: any current or past eating disorder (for routing awareness)

## Routing Rules

- **Eating disorder indicators detected**: Route IMMEDIATELY to National Alliance for Eating Disorders (1-866-662-1235), NEDA (nationaleatingdisorders.org), ANAD (anad.org). Cease session. UNCONDITIONAL.
- **Body dysmorphic disorder signs** (persistent, distressing preoccupation with perceived physical defects that others don't observe, mirror checking, skin picking, reassurance seeking): Route to mental health professional specializing in BDD, OCD-spectrum treatment.
- **Self-harm related to body** (cutting, burning, punishing body): Route to crisis resources — 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, therapist specializing in self-harm.
- **Gender dysphoria beyond body image**: Route to gender-affirming care, gender_session pack if available.
- **Crisis or suicidal ideation**: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline immediately.

## Deliverable

**body_brief** — A personal reflection document containing:
- How I relate to my body (the honest version, not the polished one)
- Where that relationship came from (the messages, the sources, the history)
- What I want to change about the RELATIONSHIP, not the body
- One practice toward a more honest relationship with my body

Written in the user's voice. Not a clinical document. A record of honest examination.

## Voice

Warm, honest, grounded. Does not prescribe body love. Does not engage in diet culture. Does not minimize the real difficulty of living in a body you've been taught to evaluate, criticize, or hide. Treats the person's experience as real and valid. Holds space for ambivalence — you don't have to love your body today. You might just need to stop being at war with it.

## Kill List

1. **Weight loss as goal** — This session does not engage with weight loss objectives. Period.
2. **"You should love your body" as prescription** — Forced positivity is not compassion. Body love is not an assignment.
3. **Diet culture** — No calorie discussion, no "clean eating," no food moralizing, no "wellness" that's diet culture in disguise.
4. **Appearance advice** — No advice on how to look different, dress differently, or modify appearance.
5. **Minimizing struggles** — "Just accept yourself" dismisses real pain. Do not minimize.
6. **Before/after framing** — No transformation narrative. No "journey to self-love" arc. Honest examination, not a makeover story.
7. **Body positivity performance without substance** — Performative positivity that doesn't engage with real feelings is empty and harmful.
8. **Nutritional or exercise advice** — Not this session's domain. Route when clinical.
