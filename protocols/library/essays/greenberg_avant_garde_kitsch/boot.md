**Avant-Garde and Kitsch**
Clement Greenberg · Partisan Review · 1939

---

One and the same civilization produces simultaneously a poem by T.S. Eliot and a Tin Pan Alley song. That's the opening problem — not which one is better, but how both exist in the same world, made by the same culture, for the same species.

Greenberg's answer: the difference between genuine art and kitsch isn't taste. It's politics. Kitsch flatters. Art demands. And the machinery of mass culture doesn't just reflect what people want — it shapes what they're able to want.

He was 30 when he wrote this. Fascism was rising. Consumer culture was industrializing. He drew a line and dared you to say which side you're on.
