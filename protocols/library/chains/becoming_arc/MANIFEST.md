## Purpose

Governs the multi-session arc for intentional self-construction. Most people live reactively — responding to circumstances, meeting expectations, drifting toward defaults. The Becoming Arc is for the person who has decided to be deliberate about who they are becoming. It is not self-improvement. Self-improvement assumes the current self is deficient and needs fixing. Becoming assumes the current self is the raw material for something that has not yet been built — and that the building requires clarity, honesty, and courage in a specific sequence.

The arc moves through six sessions that form a complete passage: clarifying what you actually value (not what you think you should value), articulating a personal mission that aligns with those values, inventorying the fears that will resist that mission, auditing your current life against the person you intend to become, setting goals that bridge the gap, and marking the transition with a deliberate rite of passage. Each session builds on the previous. The vault carries forward the cumulative self-knowledge so that no session starts from scratch and no insight evaporates between sessions.

This is the most demanding arc in the system. It asks the person to be honest about things most people avoid — what they actually care about, what they are afraid of, where their life does not match their stated values. It does not coddle. It does not flatter. It treats the person as capable of the truth about themselves, and it holds them to that standard across six sessions.

---

## Arc Purpose

The Becoming Arc exists because there is a gap between intention and action in most human lives. People know what they value but do not live accordingly. They have dreams but do not pursue them. They identify fears but do not face them. They set goals but do not follow through. The gap is not laziness or weakness — it is structural. Without a disciplined process that moves from values through mission through fears through honest assessment through concrete planning through ritual marking, the gap persists. This arc closes it — or at least makes it visible enough that the person can no longer pretend it does not exist.

The arc does not produce a deliverable in the traditional sense. It produces a person who has done the work of knowing themselves and choosing deliberately. The vault record is the artifact — a cumulative document of self-knowledge that the person can return to, revise, and build upon indefinitely.

## Session Sequence

### Session 1: Values Clarification
**Pack:** values_clarification
**Purpose:** Strip away the aspirational values — what you think you should care about — and get to the operational values: what you actually prioritize, as demonstrated by how you spend your time, money, and attention. This session is the foundation. Everything else inherits from it.
**Vault writes:** core_values, stated_vs_operational_gap, values_tensions, values_history

### Session 2: Personal Mission
**Pack:** personal_mission
**Purpose:** Translate clarified values into a personal mission statement — not a corporate mission statement for one person, but a clear articulation of what you are building your life around and why. The mission must be honest enough that it would survive scrutiny from someone who knows you well.
**Vault reads:** core_values, values_tensions from S1
**Vault writes:** mission_statement, mission_values_alignment, mission_scope, mission_non_negotiables

### Session 3: Fear Inventory
**Pack:** fear_inventory
**Purpose:** Every mission has opposition, and the most formidable opposition is internal. This session inventories the fears that will resist the mission — fear of failure, fear of success, fear of judgment, fear of loss, fear of inadequacy. Naming fears does not eliminate them, but it prevents them from operating unseen.
**Vault reads:** mission_statement, core_values from prior sessions
**Vault writes:** fear_inventory, fear_patterns, fear_mission_interference, fear_origin_stories

### Session 4: Life Audit
**Pack:** life_audit
**Purpose:** The honest accounting. How does your current life — relationships, work, habits, environment, commitments — align with your values and mission? Where is the gap? This session does not judge the gap. It maps it. The person cannot close a gap they cannot see.
**Vault reads:** core_values, mission_statement, fear_inventory from prior sessions
**Vault writes:** life_audit_results, alignment_areas, misalignment_areas, structural_obstacles, resource_inventory

### Session 5: Goal Setting
**Pack:** goal_setting_session
**Purpose:** Now that values are clear, mission is articulated, fears are named, and the life audit has revealed the gap — set goals that close it. Goals at this point in the arc are qualitatively different from goals set without this foundation. They are grounded, honest, and connected to something the person actually cares about.
**Vault reads:** full arc vault — core_values, mission_statement, fear_inventory, life_audit_results, misalignment_areas
**Vault writes:** goals, goal_values_alignment, goal_fear_awareness, first_steps, accountability_structure

### Session 6: Rite of Passage
**Pack:** rite_of_passage
**Purpose:** Mark the transition. Becoming is not complete when goals are set — it is marked when the person acknowledges that they have crossed a threshold. The rite of passage session creates a deliberate moment of recognition: you have done the work, you know who you are becoming, and this moment marks the beginning of that life.
**Vault reads:** full arc vault
**Vault writes:** rite_description, threshold_crossed, commitment_made, arc_reflection

## Vault Inheritance Map

The Becoming Arc has the most dense vault inheritance of any arc because each session genuinely depends on everything that came before.

- **S1 → S2:** core_values, values_tensions — mission must be built on actual values, not aspirational ones
- **S1+S2 → S3:** core_values, mission_statement — fear inventory is specifically about fears that threaten the mission grounded in these values
- **S1+S2+S3 → S4:** core_values, mission_statement, fear_inventory — life audit checks alignment against values and mission while accounting for the fears that maintain misalignment
- **S1+S2+S3+S4 → S5:** full vault — goal setting draws on everything: values (what matters), mission (where heading), fears (what will resist), audit (where you currently are)
- **All → S6:** full vault — the rite of passage integrates the entire arc into a single moment of marking

## Entry Session Protocol

The arc always enters at Session 1 (values_clarification). The Becoming Arc does not permit mid-arc entry because the entire sequence depends on the foundation laid in S1. A person who says they already know their values is told: the structured process reveals things self-knowledge misses. Every time.

Entry assessment captures: what prompted the desire for intentional self-construction, current life stage, any prior personal development or therapeutic work, and what the person hopes will be different after completing the arc. This context is written to vault for downstream sessions.

The entry session also screens for appropriateness. The Becoming Arc is not for someone in acute crisis — they need stabilization first. It is not for someone seeking therapy — they need a therapist. It is for someone who is stable enough to do hard introspective work and motivated enough to sustain it across six sessions.

---

## Adaptation Rules

- Sessions 1-6 are sequential and mandatory. The Becoming Arc is not a buffet — it is a curriculum. However, pacing between sessions adapts to the person.
- If values clarification in S1 reveals the person is in a values crisis (they genuinely do not know what they care about) → S1 may be extended or repeated before proceeding.
- If fear inventory in S3 surfaces trauma → recommend therapeutic support alongside the arc. The arc does not process trauma.
- If life audit in S4 reveals the person's life is significantly misaligned with their values → this is the hardest session. Allow extra time. Do not minimize the gap.
- If goal setting in S5 produces goals that do not align with the values from S1 → flag the misalignment explicitly. The person may have changed their values during the arc. If so, revisit S1 for reclarification.
- If the person is not ready for the rite of passage in S6 → the arc can wait. The rite is meaningful only when the person is ready to cross the threshold.

---

## Completion Criteria

The arc is complete when:
1. Values have been clarified and the person recognizes them as authentically their own
2. A personal mission has been articulated that the person would stake their name on
3. Fears have been inventoried and their relationship to the mission is understood
4. The life audit has been completed honestly — alignment and misalignment both acknowledged
5. Goals have been set that are grounded in values, informed by the audit, and aware of the fears that will resist them
6. The rite of passage has been conducted and the person has marked the threshold

---

## Mandatory Routing

- Acute mental health crisis → do not enter arc, provide stabilization resources
- Trauma surfacing during fear inventory or life audit → recommend therapeutic support alongside
- Suicidal ideation at any point → crisis resources + stay present
- Substance use as coping → note and recommend professional assessment
- The person realizes mid-arc they need grief processing, not becoming → route to grief_arc
