# Exit Interview — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The `form_exit_interview` pack guides users through conducting and documenting a structured exit interview with a departing employee. Exit interviews are a valuable HR practice that provides organizations with candid feedback about workplace culture, management effectiveness, role satisfaction, and reasons for voluntary turnover. This pack produces a comprehensive exit interview document capturing the employee's perspective across multiple dimensions. The pack assists with form completion and interview structuring only — it does NOT provide legal advice regarding separation agreements, final pay requirements, or employment disputes.

## Scope

This pack covers the complete exit interview documentation process, from basic employee identification and employment history through detailed qualitative feedback on multiple aspects of the employment experience. It captures the primary reason for departure, positive aspects of the work environment, areas the organization could improve, feedback on direct management, and the employee's willingness to return or recommend the organization. The pack does not handle COBRA notifications, final paycheck processing, benefits termination, return of company property, or any other offboarding logistics — it is focused exclusively on the interview documentation.

## Autonomy Level

**ZERO** — The assistant collects and documents the departing employee's responses but takes no autonomous action. All information is presented for review without any submission, forwarding, or action-taking. This is appropriate because exit interview data often contains sensitive feedback about specific managers, teams, or organizational practices, and must be handled with discretion. The deployer determines how the documented feedback is used within the organization.

## Turn Budget

**8-10 turns.** Exit interviews benefit from a conversational, open-ended approach that encourages candor. The assistant should create space for the departing employee to share their perspective without rushing through a checklist. However, the conversation should remain structured enough to ensure all key topics are covered systematically.

## Required Fields

### Employee Identification

- **Employee Name**: Full name as used in company records.
- **Employee Title/Position**: The role held at time of departure.
- **Department**: Organizational unit or team.
- **Hire Date**: When the employee joined the organization.
- **Last Day of Employment**: Final working day.
- **Tenure**: Calculated duration of employment.

### Departure Context

- **Primary Reason for Leaving**: The main driver behind the decision to leave. Common categories include:
  - New opportunity (better role, compensation, career growth)
  - Relocation
  - Personal/family reasons
  - Dissatisfaction with role, management, culture, or compensation
  - Return to school
  - Retirement
  - Health reasons
  - Involuntary separation

  The assistant should capture both the category and the employee's own words describing their reasoning. Multiple factors are common and should all be documented.

### Positive Feedback — What Worked Well

This section captures what the employee valued about their time with the organization. The assistant should explore multiple dimensions:

- **Role and responsibilities**: Was the work itself engaging and aligned with expectations?
- **Team and colleagues**: Quality of working relationships and collaboration.
- **Culture and values**: Did the organization's culture align with the employee's values?
- **Growth and development**: Were there opportunities to learn, develop skills, and advance?
- **Compensation and benefits**: Was the total rewards package competitive and fair?
- **Work-life balance**: Was the balance sustainable?
- **Resources and tools**: Were adequate resources provided to do the job effectively?

The assistant should prompt for specific examples rather than accepting only general positive statements.

### Areas for Improvement

This section captures what the employee believes the organization could do better. The same dimensions apply — role design, team dynamics, culture, growth opportunities, compensation, work-life balance, and resources. The assistant should create a safe conversational space for honest feedback and probe for specificity. This is often the most valuable section of an exit interview.

### Manager Feedback

Specific feedback about the employee's direct manager or supervisor:

- **Management style**: How would the employee describe their manager's approach?
- **Communication**: Was communication clear, timely, and two-directional?
- **Support and development**: Did the manager support the employee's growth?
- **Recognition**: Was the employee's work acknowledged appropriately?
- **Fairness**: Was the manager fair and consistent in expectations and treatment?

This section should be documented carefully with the understanding that it will be visible to HR leadership. The assistant should note whether the employee consents to their feedback being shared with the manager directly.

### Future Relationship

- **Would Return**: Would the employee consider returning to the organization in the future? Under what conditions?
- **Would Recommend**: Would the employee recommend the organization as an employer to others? Why or why not?

These are straightforward yes/no/conditional questions with space for elaboration. They serve as useful aggregate metrics when tracked across multiple exit interviews.

### Additional Comments

An open-ended section for anything the employee wants to share that was not covered by the structured questions. This can include advice for their successor, observations about industry trends affecting the organization, or personal reflections on their career journey.

## Conversation Flow

1. **Greeting and context**: Explain the purpose of the exit interview, assure the employee that feedback is valued and will be used constructively. Note this is not a legal proceeding.
2. **Employee identification**: Collect name, title, department, dates, tenure.
3. **Reason for leaving**: Explore the primary reason and any contributing factors.
4. **What worked well**: Walk through what the employee valued about the experience.
5. **Areas for improvement**: Explore what the organization could do better.
6. **Manager feedback**: Collect specific feedback on direct management.
7. **Future relationship**: Would return? Would recommend?
8. **Additional comments**: Open floor for anything not yet covered.
9. **Review and confirmation**: Present the complete document for accuracy check.
10. **Deliverable generation**: Produce the completed exit interview document.

## Guardrails

- This pack does NOT provide legal advice regarding separation, severance, or disputes.
- The assistant must not make judgments about the validity of the employee's feedback.
- The assistant must not promise anonymity — the deployer controls how feedback is used.
- The assistant should not probe into legally sensitive areas (discrimination claims, harassment allegations) — those require formal channels and legal counsel.
- The assistant should maintain a neutral, receptive tone regardless of the feedback's content.
- If the employee expresses distress, the assistant should acknowledge it professionally and continue, not attempt counseling.

## Deliverable

A completed exit interview document containing all sections, formatted professionally for HR review. The document should be suitable for inclusion in the employee's personnel file and for aggregation with other exit interview data to identify organizational trends.
