## Static — Authored Domain Knowledge

### Core Investment Metrics

Every real estate analysis bottoms out in a handful of metrics. Know them cold:

- **Cap Rate** (Capitalization Rate) — NOI / Purchase Price. Market-rate return on a property if bought all-cash. Typical ranges: 4-6% (A-class urban), 6-8% (B-class suburban), 8-12% (C-class / value-add). Lower cap = higher price relative to income.
- **Cash-on-Cash Return** — Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested. The leveraged return on actual dollars deployed. Differs from cap rate because it accounts for financing. Target: 8-12% for most buy-and-hold investors.
- **GRM** (Gross Rent Multiplier) — Purchase Price / Annual Gross Rent. Quick screening metric. Lower GRM = faster payback. Typical: 8-15 for residential, varies widely by market.
- **NOI** (Net Operating Income) — Gross Rent - Vacancy - Operating Expenses. Does NOT include debt service. This is the foundational number. Get it wrong and everything downstream is wrong.
- **DSCR** (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) — NOI / Annual Debt Service. Lender metric. Below 1.0 means the property doesn't cover its mortgage from operations. Most lenders require 1.20-1.25 minimum.
- **IRR** (Internal Rate of Return) — Time-weighted total return including cash flow, appreciation, principal paydown, and exit. The most comprehensive metric but also the most assumption-dependent.
- **ARV** (After-Repair Value) — Projected value after renovations. Central to flip analysis. Should be supported by comparable sales, not hope.

### Investment Strategy Profiles

Investors cluster into recognizable strategies. The first few sentences usually signal which one:

- **Buy-and-hold** — "rental income," "cash flow," "passive income," "long-term," "portfolio." Cares about: cap rate, cash-on-cash, DSCR, cash flow stability.
- **Fix-and-flip** — "rehab," "flip," "ARV," "renovation," "resale." Cares about: total project cost, ARV accuracy, holding period, profit margin, speed.
- **BRRRR** — "buy, rehab, rent, refinance, repeat." Hybrid: flip-quality renovation + hold for cash flow. Cares about: ARV for refinance, cash-out amount, stabilized cash flow post-refi.
- **House hacking** — "live in one unit," "duplex," "FHA," "owner-occupied." Cares about: net housing cost, FHA eligibility, rental offset.
- **Appreciation play** — "equity growth," "emerging market," "gentrification," "long-term upside." Cares about: market trends, comparable trajectories, entry price vs. replacement cost.
- **1031 exchange** — "tax-deferred," "exchange," "like-kind." Cares about: identification timelines (45 days), closing deadlines (180 days), qualified intermediary requirements.

### Buyer Profile Patterns

- **First-time investor** — needs education on metrics, conservative assumptions, clear risk disclosure. Don't assume familiarity with cap rates or DSCR.
- **Experienced investor** — speaks in metrics, wants efficiency, may push back on assumptions. Respect their knowledge but verify inputs.
- **Institutional / syndication** — large-scale, sophisticated modeling expectations. IRR-driven, multiple exit scenarios, sensitivity tables.
- **Owner-occupant** — emotional component alongside financial. Lifestyle factors matter. Don't over-financialize a home purchase.
- **Out-of-state investor** — needs market context they can't get from driving around. Management costs are real, not optional.

### Common Expense Ratios and Defaults

Standard operating expense assumptions for residential investment properties:

- **Vacancy** — 5-10% depending on market and property class. 8% is a reasonable default. Zero vacancy is a red flag in any analysis.
- **Property management** — 8-12% of gross rent. Self-management is "free" until it isn't. Always model it.
- **Maintenance reserve** — 5-10% of gross rent. Older properties trend higher. Deferred maintenance is a hidden liability.
- **Insurance** — 0.4-0.7% of property value annually. Flood zones, coastal, and disaster-prone areas significantly higher.
- **Property taxes** — highly local. 0.5% (Hawaii) to 2.5%+ (Texas, New Jersey). Always use actual assessed rates.
- **CapEx reserve** — separate from maintenance. Roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical. Rule of thumb: $200-400/unit/month depending on age.
- **Closing costs** — Buy side: 2-4% (lender fees, title, escrow, inspection). Sell side: 5-7% (agent commissions, transfer taxes, title).

### Market Analysis Signals

- **Price-to-rent ratio** — Purchase price / Annual rent. Below 15: generally favors buying. Above 20: generally favors renting. Between 15-20: depends on circumstances.
- **Days on market** — rising DOM signals cooling market. Declining DOM signals heating market. Affects negotiation leverage.
- **Inventory months** — Active listings / monthly sales. Below 3: seller's market. Above 6: buyer's market. 3-6: balanced.
- **Rental yield compression** — when cap rates fall below financing costs, cash flow turns negative. This is a market timing signal, not a property defect.
- **Property tax reassessment risk** — purchase can trigger reassessment to market value, dramatically increasing the tax bill in capped-increase jurisdictions.

### Flip-Specific Knowledge

- **70% rule** — maximum purchase price = (ARV x 0.70) - rehab costs. Quick screening filter. Experienced flippers may stretch to 75% in hot markets.
- **Holding cost burn rate** — Monthly cost of capital + insurance + taxes + utilities on a non-revenue property. Time is the enemy of flip margins.
- **Scope creep** — rehab budgets routinely exceed estimates by 15-25%. Build contingency into every projection.
- **Permit timelines** — varies by municipality. Can add 2-8 weeks to project timeline. Factor into holding cost projections.
