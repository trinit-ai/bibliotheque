# Astrophotography Pack — MANIFEST

## Overview

The Astrophotography pack provides a conversational companion for photographing the night sky. It covers the full workflow from planning and equipment selection through camera settings, field technique, and post-processing. The pack serves photographers at all levels — from someone with a DSLR or mirrorless camera who wants to capture their first Milky Way image, to intermediate astrophotographers working on tracked deep-sky imaging, star trails, or time-lapse sequences.

## Purpose and Scope

Astrophotography is one of the most rewarding and technically demanding forms of photography. It combines astronomy (knowing what is in the sky and when), optics (understanding focal length, aperture, and sensor behavior), exposure science (managing extremely low light with long exposures), and post-processing (extracting signal from noise). The learning curve is steep, but the results — capturing the Milky Way arcing over a landscape, revealing nebula structure invisible to the naked eye, or recording meteor streaks across a star field — are uniquely compelling.

The pack addresses the most common barrier to entry: knowing where to start and what settings to use. Many photographers attempt astrophotography with their existing gear, take a few disappointing images, and give up — not because their equipment is inadequate, but because they did not understand the specific technical requirements of night sky imaging. The pack breaks these requirements down into understandable, actionable guidance.

**Widefield and Landscape Astrophotography**: This is where most people start — a camera on a tripod, a wide-angle lens, and the night sky. The pack covers the 500 Rule (and its more accurate NPF Rule variant) for determining maximum exposure time before stars trail. Lens selection — fast, wide primes (14mm f/2.8, 24mm f/1.4, 20mm f/1.8) as the workhorses of the genre. ISO selection and noise management — why modern cameras perform well at ISO 3200-6400 and how to evaluate your camera's usable ISO range. Focus technique — infinity focus in the dark using live view magnification, focus peaking, or manual focus marks. Composition — foreground interest, leading lines, framing the Milky Way core, panoramic stitching for wider fields.

**Star Trails**: Long-exposure or stacked sequences showing Earth's rotation as circular arcs around Polaris (Northern Hemisphere) or the South Celestial Pole. The pack covers single-exposure vs. stacked approaches, interval timing, gap avoidance, and stacking software (StarStaX, Sequator). Composition considerations unique to star trails — Polaris placement, foreground lighting, exposure length vs. trail density.

**Tracked Deep-Sky Imaging**: For users with star trackers (iOptron SkyGuider, Sky-Watcher Star Adventurer) or equatorial mounts, the pack discusses longer exposures, narrower fields of view, and the targets that become accessible with tracking — Andromeda Galaxy, Orion Nebula, North America Nebula, various star clusters. Polar alignment technique, autoguiding basics, and the integration of multiple sub-exposures.

**Time-Lapse and Meteor Photography**: Interval shooting for night sky time-lapse sequences. Holy-grail time-lapse (sunset to stars transitions with ramped exposure). Meteor shower photography — wide fields, continuous shooting, and the statistics of capture.

**Planning**: Moon phase and its impact on sky darkness. Milky Way core visibility by month and latitude. Weather forecast interpretation for clear sky prediction (Clear Outside, Astrospheric as planning tools). Light pollution maps and dark sky site selection. Astronomical twilight timing. Planning apps (PhotoPills, Stellarium, PlanIt Pro) for previsualing compositions.

**Post-Processing**: The pack covers the essential post-processing workflow for astrophotography. White balance correction for night skies. Noise reduction approaches. Light pollution gradient removal. Stacking multiple exposures (Sequator, DeepSkyStacker, Siril). Selective adjustments — bringing out Milky Way structure, enhancing nebula color, managing star size. The pack discusses both Lightroom/Photoshop workflows and dedicated astro processing software.

## Session Flow

Sessions run 8 to 10 turns. The pack opens by asking what the user wants to photograph and what equipment they have. Camera body and lens inventory is essential context — recommendations depend heavily on sensor size, available focal lengths, and maximum apertures. Experience level determines whether the conversation starts with basic exposure settings or jumps to advanced techniques like tracked stacking or narrowband imaging.

## What This Pack Does Not Do

The pack does not cover daytime photography or general photography technique beyond what applies directly to astrophotography. It does not cover telescope-based imaging through eyepiece projection or prime focus (though tracked camera-lens setups are covered). It does not provide real-time sky conditions or weather forecasts. It does not generate processing presets or automated editing.

## Zero-Consequence Design

Astrophotography carries no safety risk. The pack may mention practical night shooting considerations (dressing warmly, bringing a red headlamp, being aware of your surroundings in dark locations, wildlife awareness in remote areas) but these are practical suggestions, not safety-critical.

## Integration Notes

Standard protocol loading via assembler. No data rail gates required. Sessions log to inbox under per-turn upsert. No external tools or APIs.
