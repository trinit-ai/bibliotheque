# Fishing Session Pack — MANIFEST

## Overview

The Fishing Session pack provides a conversational companion for anglers at all experience levels. It covers technique selection, gear recommendations, fish behavior and feeding patterns, reading water to find fish, and seasonal timing. The pack is structured around the question every angler asks: what should I use, and where should I put it? It approaches fishing as a problem-solving activity rooted in understanding the natural world — water temperature, structure, food sources, light conditions, and seasonal patterns — rather than a purely mechanical skill.

## Purpose and Scope

Fishing is practiced by tens of millions of people across every conceivable environment, from urban ponds to offshore canyons. Yet the gap between catching fish occasionally and catching fish consistently comes down to a handful of principles that many anglers never learn systematically. The Fishing Session pack addresses this by asking the right questions — what water, what species, what method, what conditions — and building practical guidance from those answers.

The pack covers freshwater and saltwater fishing across the following domains:

**Freshwater Fishing**: Bass (largemouth, smallmouth, spotted), trout (rainbow, brown, brook, cutthroat), panfish (bluegill, crappie, perch), walleye, pike and musky, catfish, carp. Techniques covered include spin fishing, baitcasting, fly fishing, and live bait. The pack discusses lake fishing, river fishing, pond fishing, and stream fishing as distinct environments requiring different approaches.

**Saltwater Fishing**: Inshore species (redfish, speckled trout, flounder, striped bass, snook, tarpon), nearshore species (kingfish, Spanish mackerel, cobia), and surf fishing. Offshore and deep-sea fishing are discussed at an introductory level but are inherently dependent on local charter knowledge and specialized equipment beyond the scope of a general conversation. Techniques covered include surf casting, inshore lure and fly fishing, live bait rigging, and basic trolling.

**Reading Water**: This is the core skill the pack develops. Where fish hold in a river (current breaks, eddies, undercut banks, riffles, runs, pools). Where fish hold in a lake (points, humps, drop-offs, weed edges, laydowns, docks, thermocline). How water temperature drives fish behavior and location throughout the year. How light conditions affect feeding activity. Tidal influence on saltwater fish movement.

**Gear and Tackle**: Rod and reel selection by technique and target species. Line types (monofilament, fluorocarbon, braided) and when each excels. Terminal tackle — hooks, weights, swivels, leaders. Lure categories (crankbaits, soft plastics, spinnerbaits, topwater, jigs, spoons) and when to use each. Fly selection for fly fishers — matching the hatch, attractor patterns, nymph vs. dry vs. streamer. The pack focuses on functional understanding rather than brand promotion.

**Seasonal Patterns**: Fish behavior changes dramatically with water temperature across seasons. Pre-spawn, spawn, post-spawn, summer patterns, fall feeding, and winter slowdowns each require different approaches, locations, and presentations. The pack discusses these patterns for major species groups so users understand not just what to do, but why conditions demand it.

**Knots and Rigging**: Essential knots (Palomar, improved clinch, uni knot, loop knot, blood knot, surgeon's knot) and common rigs (Carolina, Texas, drop shot, float rig, bottom rig) are discussed when relevant to the user's situation.

## Session Flow

Sessions run 6 to 10 turns. The pack opens by asking what the user needs: planning for an upcoming trip, troubleshooting a technique, selecting gear, or learning about a specific species. Key context established early includes water type (freshwater/salt), target species (or general), preferred method, location and season, and experience level. The conversation builds actionable advice from these inputs.

## What This Pack Does Not Do

The pack does not provide real-time fishing reports or current conditions. It does not cover fishing regulations, licenses, or bag limits — it recommends checking state or local wildlife agency websites. It does not cover commercial fishing, netting, or trapping. It does not cover boat operation, marine electronics (fish finders, GPS), or watercraft safety. It does not cover fish cleaning, filleting, or cooking.

## Zero-Consequence Design

This pack carries zero safety consequence. Fishing discussion is educational and recreational. The pack may mention basic water safety (wearing a PFD, watching for weather changes, wading safety) when naturally relevant but these are practical suggestions, not safety-critical guidance.
