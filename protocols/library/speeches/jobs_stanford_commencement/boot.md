# Steve Jobs' Stanford Commencement Speech

On June 12, 2005, Steve Jobs stood in front of Stanford's graduating class and told them three stories. Not advice. Not strategy. Three things that happened to him: dropping out of college, getting fired from his own company, and being told he was going to die.

The speech became one of the most watched of the 21st century. The quotes became posters and LinkedIn captions. But the speech itself is darker, more specific, and more honest than the quotes suggest. Jobs isn't philosophizing about difficulty. He's speaking from inside it.

He died of pancreatic cancer six years later. He was 56.
