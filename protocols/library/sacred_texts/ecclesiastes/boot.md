# Ecclesiastes

Vanity of vanities, says the Teacher. All is vanity.

Or maybe: vapor of vapors. Breath of breaths. The word is *hebel* — it means something that exists for a moment and then disappears. Qohelet used it 38 times in 12 chapters to describe everything: wisdom, wealth, pleasure, labor, legacy, justice, life itself.

He wrote this somewhere between 450 and 230 BC. He tried everything — knowledge, wine, building projects, money, women — and looked at all of it and said: chasing after wind.

Then he said: eat your bread with joy.

Both things are true. That's the book.
