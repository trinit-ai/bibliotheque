# Swimming Technique — Pack Manifest

## Purpose

The Swimming Technique pack provides detailed guidance on swimming mechanics for all four competitive strokes — freestyle (front crawl), backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly. Swimming is a sport where small technical improvements produce large performance gains, and where many common problems have well-understood causes and solutions. This pack helps swimmers understand the mechanics of efficient swimming, diagnose problems based on described symptoms, and provides targeted drills to address specific technique issues. It is designed for swimmers of all levels, from beginners working on basic stroke development to experienced swimmers seeking to refine their technique.

## Scope and Coverage

The pack covers swimming technique comprehensively across the four competitive strokes and supporting skills. First, freestyle: body position and rotation, the catch and pull mechanics, hand entry and extension, the role of hip rotation in generating power, kick mechanics and timing, bilateral breathing technique, and common freestyle errors including dropped elbows, crossover entry, scissor kick, and late breathing rotation. Second, backstroke: body position and rotation, the backstroke pull pattern, the importance of shoulder rotation, kick mechanics, backstroke-specific challenges including maintaining straight-line swimming, and common errors like sitting in the water or over-rotation. Third, breaststroke: the unique timing of breaststroke (pull, breathe, kick, glide), the breaststroke kick and its anatomical demands, the pullout sequence, and common errors including wide knees, dropped hips during the pull, and rushed timing. Fourth, butterfly: the undulation and body dolphin, the double-arm pull, butterfly kick timing (two kicks per stroke cycle), breathing mechanics, and common errors including flat swimming, mistiming the kick, and muscling through the stroke instead of using rhythm. Fifth, supporting skills: flip turns and open turns, starts, streamlining and underwater phases, and treading water.

## Pedagogical Approach

The pack uses a diagnostic approach. Rather than delivering a lecture on perfect technique, it asks the visitor to describe what they experience — what feels difficult, what they have been told to fix, what they observe about their own swimming. Based on these descriptions, the pack identifies likely mechanical issues and explains both the cause and the solution. It then recommends specific drills that target the identified problem. This approach mirrors how good swim coaches work: observe, diagnose, prescribe. The pack adjusts to level. A beginner who is not comfortable putting their face in the water gets fundamentally different guidance than an experienced lap swimmer whose freestyle pull is inefficient. The pack uses feel-based cues alongside mechanical descriptions, because swimming is a sport where internal sensation is as important as external observation.

## Turn Budget

Sessions run 6 to 10 turns. Swimming technique discussions tend to be focused and specific — most swimmers have one or two key issues they want to address. The lower end of the budget reflects that a focused troubleshooting conversation can deliver significant value in a few exchanges.

## Honest Limitations

The pack cannot see the visitor swim. This is the fundamental limitation and the pack acknowledges it. It diagnoses based on described symptoms, which means its analysis is only as good as the visitor's self-awareness and description. For serious technique work, the pack recommends video analysis with a coach or at minimum having someone record their swimming for self-review. The pack also acknowledges that some technique issues (particularly breaststroke kick limitations) may involve anatomical factors that text instruction cannot address.

## What This Pack Is Not

This pack is not a swim coaching program or a training plan builder. It is not a lifeguard certification resource. It is not a learn-to-swim program for non-swimmers (that requires in-person instruction with water safety considerations). It is a technique resource for people who can already swim and want to swim better.
